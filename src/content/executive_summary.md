## Key Findings

1. **Two funds, not one**: Massachusetts operates two parallel technology funds for registries of deeds:
   - **Fund 0049** ("Registers Technological Fund") — for the 15 state-controlled registry districts, administered by the Secretary of the Commonwealth
   - **Fund 0050** ("County Registers Techological Fund") — for the 6 county-controlled registry districts (Barnstable, Bristol, Dukes, Norfolk, Plymouth, Nantucket)

2. **$151.9 million** has been spent across both funds from FY2005 through FY2026 (partial). Adjusted for inflation, this is **$207.1 million in 2026 dollars**.

3. **Peak spending** occurred in FY2010 ($11.8M combined) and FY2014-2015 ($10.8M and $9.6M), reflecting major IT infrastructure investments and backfile scanning pushes.

4. **Revenue totals $159.5 million** over the same period, generated by a $5 per-recording surcharge at all registries.

5. **FY2002-FY2004 remain unquantified**. The funds were created in March 2003, so FY2002 had zero activity. FY2003-FY2004 actual spending data would require manual extraction from Comptroller SBFR reports.

6. **Direct scanning/imaging** (narrow classification from CTHRU transaction data) accounts for only $3.3M of the $61.8M in Fund 0049 FY2010-2026 spending. The vast majority of fund expenditures support IT infrastructure, contracted IT staff, and equipment maintenance for the MassLandRecords platform.

- **HIGH**: Fund-level annual totals (FY2005-2026) are from official Comptroller data
- **HIGH**: Transaction-level data completeness within Fund 0049 (FY2010-2026)
- **MODERATE**: Narrow/broad classification relies on MMARS Object codes; some scanning work may be bundled into IT service contracts
- **NOT AVAILABLE**: FY2002-2004 data; transaction-level detail for Fund 0050
